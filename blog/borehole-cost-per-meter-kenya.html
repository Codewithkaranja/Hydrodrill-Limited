Borehole Drilling Cost Per Meter in Kenya (2026 Guide)

People ask “how much per meter?” — but pricing is determined by depth, geology, casing standards, test pumping and the pump system (electric or solar).

What affects borehole drilling cost per meter?

  • Depth & geology: harder formations increase time and wear.
  • Casing & screens: quality materials protect yield and lifespan.
  • Test pumping: confirms sustainable yield and recovery.
  • Pump + power: solar systems add panels, controllers, structures.
  • Access & logistics: site location and terrain.

Full system cost breakdown (Survey → Tap)

  1. Hydrogeological survey & siting
  2. Drilling + casing + gravel pack
  3. Well development + test pumping
  4. Water quality testing (recommended)
  5. Pump installation (electric/solar) + controls
  6. Tank tower + storage + distribution

FAQs

Is “cheap per meter” always good?

No. Low pricing can mean weak casing, skipped testing, or poor development, which risks low yield or early failure.

Do you handle solar pumping systems?
