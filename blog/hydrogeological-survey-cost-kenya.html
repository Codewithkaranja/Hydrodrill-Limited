Hydrogeological Survey Cost in Kenya

A hydrogeological survey helps identify the best drilling point, estimate depth/yield, and reduce the risk of spending on a low-performing borehole.

What’s included in a hydrogeological survey?

  • Site assessment and geology review
  • Geophysical testing (e.g., resistivity profiling)
  • Proposed drilling point(s) and depth estimate
  • Expected yield range + risks
  • Survey report for planning/approvals

What affects survey pricing?

  • Site size and number of test points
  • Accessibility and travel distance
  • Geology complexity
  • Urgency and reporting requirements

How this improves drilling success

Survey data informs proper casing/screens, drilling approach, and realistic expectations—especially in semi-arid zones.

FAQs

Can I drill without a survey?

You can, but risk increases. Surveys reduce guesswork and costly drilling failures.

Do you provide the report?

Yes — we provide documentation to support planning and project execution.
